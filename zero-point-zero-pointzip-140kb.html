Zero Point

November 6, 2025
Zero Point is the cheesy first map name that I chose to indicate my journey through the world of level design while also learning environment art. Choosing Doom as the medium for developing those skills was the perfect starting point, and building it on the foundations of the first episode maps was a logical choice for me. Zero Point is a mix of the classic tech-base theme with a twist of non-linear progression.

The level structure at the start was unclear, but following the standard corridor-room-corridor-room flow and including reachable outside areas guided me through the process. The themes of each room vary between personnel accommodations, tech labs, nukage chambers, and more. Being a beginner, I had almost no specific plan for each room at first, so despite having a concept, most of the rooms were iterated separately without properly considering the organic flow of the map. I simply applied landmark pieces of level design that made each room unique in its own way, so there is a bit of everything all around.

The visual part was, if not equally, even more challenging than the level design. Trying to be pixel-perfect and experimenting with different textures took a large amount of time due to the limitations of the format I initially chose, which affected the level design considerably. Later in the project, the format (Doom) was changed to another with much more freedom (UDMF) for texturing. The monsters were also filtered to better fit each room‚Äôs theme, more tech and humanoid monsters in the tech bases, and the more hellish kinds in places where dangerous experiments took place.

All things considered, the map received good reviews, especially considering it was made by someone completely new to the field. The map is not recognized for its difficulty, but rather as a love letter to the original Doom levels. I consider it a good success in those regards, especially coming from a community of seasoned veterans and creators of all types and calibers.
